News

Ocean Boulevard — a major highway serving the Long Beach-San Pedro area — re-opened at 12:11 a.m. today (Sunday), more than 28 hours ahead of schedule. Construction crews who began demolishing the last major sections of the Pier T Avenue off-ramp on Friday night were able to complete the work, remove debris and have the highway ready for commuters and truck drivers ahead of the 5 a.m. Monday (July 15) deadline.

The weekend closure affected a key stretch of Ocean Boulevard and the Gerald Desmond Bridge—a major commuter route between Long Beach, San Pedro and other South Bay communities. This section – between State – also serves as a primary road for trucks entering and leaving the Port of Long Beach. Motorists heeded warnings to avoid the area during the closure and officials reported few problems along alternate routes.

The demolition work now clears the way for SFI.
